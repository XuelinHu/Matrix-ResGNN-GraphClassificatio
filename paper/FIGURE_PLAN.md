### Figure 2. Branch-count ablation

Purpose:

- show the rise-then-fall pattern as branch count increases
- contrast the preferred branch budget on `PROTEINS` and `DD`
- compare `HorizontalRes`, `MatrixRes`, and `MatrixResGated`

Source:

- `records/LATEST/summaries/branch_ablation_summary.csv`
- generated by `scripts/generate_ablation_figures.py`

Current file:

- `figures/exp/fig_branch_count_ablation.pdf`

Paper message:

- branch growth helps at first because it increases useful diversity
- too many branches eventually weaken optimization and add redundant residual traffic
- `PROTEINS` tolerates a larger branch budget than `DD`

### Figure 3. MatrixResGated sensitivity slices

Purpose:

- show the tunable region of the controlled matrix model
- highlight that `PROTEINS` and `DD` prefer different operating regions

Source:

- `records/LATEST/summaries/parameter_sensitivity_summary.csv`
- generated by `scripts/generate_ablation_figures.py`

Current file:

- `figures/exp/fig_matrixresgated_sensitivity.pdf`

Paper message:

- `PROTEINS` benefits from mild sparsification
- `DD` prefers lighter and more conservative settings

### Figure 4. Mechanism compact figure

Purpose:

- explain why branch count first helps and then hurts
- align performance trends with a minimal mechanism story

Recommended metrics:

- `branch diversity`
- `branch cosine similarity`
- `mean gradient norm`

Source:

- `records/LATEST/summaries/mechanism_compact_summary.csv`

Planned design:

- panel A: accuracy vs `B`
- panel B: branch diversity vs `B`
- panel C: branch cosine vs `B`
- panel D: mean gradient norm vs `B`

Recommended scope:

- `PROTEINS` and `DD`
- `HorizontalRes`, `MatrixRes`, `MatrixResGated`

Why only these metrics:

- `branch diversity` captures representational spread
- `branch cosine` captures redundancy
- `mean gradient norm` captures optimization weakening

The rest should move to appendix unless a later draft shows a strong need:

- `CKA`
- `active_ratio_mean`
- `residual_count_total`
- `residual_norm_pre_total`
- `residual_norm_post_total`
